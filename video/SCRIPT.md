# Covenant — Demo Video Script

Target: ~2:20 total. Pace ~140 wpm. One narration block per scene.
Voice: composed, measured authority (DeFi/Infra).

---

## Scene 1: hook (~12s, ~28 words)

Every smart contract still waits for someone to push the button. A keeper. An oracle. A human. Covenant is an agreement that needs nobody. Not even you.

## Scene 2: problem (~16s, ~38 words)

Real deals are written in English. "Pay the freelancer when the site is live." To put that on-chain, you normally translate fuzzy reality into brittle Solidity, then babysit it with oracles and cron jobs. Covenant skips all of that.

## Scene 3: authoring (~24s, ~56 words)

You write the deal in plain English. Covenant extracts the parties, the escrow, the deadline, and the clause, and you fund it once. That is the last transaction anyone ever sends. The clause stays English — "the site is live and mentions Somnia" — because the judge can actually read.

## Scene 4: loop (~22s, ~50 words)

Here is the loop. Covenant schedules its own wake-up with Somnia's reactivity layer. At check time, the chain calls the contract. The contract sends real evidence — the live website — to a panel of validator LLMs. Each scores the clause independently. Covenant takes the median. No single judge can swing a payout.

## Scene 5: onchain (~20s, ~46 words)

This is real, on Somnia testnet. The singleton holds every schedule and one thirty-two-S-T-T buffer. Escrow is walled off — it can never be spent on gas. And every judgment links to a public receipt you can open: per-validator scores, on the Somnia agent explorer.
